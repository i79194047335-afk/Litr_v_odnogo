### A1. In-sample tick data is ~17.5–17.9% duplicated `OPEN`

The collector's dedup fix landed 2026-07-02. The calibration days are

| 20260702 | mixed | 628,848 (239,435 v1) | 39,902 (16.67% of v1 rows) |
| 20260703+ | v2 | — | 0.00% |

This is CONTEXT.md's "Open question #2" (retro-dedup), previously filed as
*low priority*. It is **not** low priority: every in-sample backtest number
and `range_size=15.3` itself were computed on these files.

What is and isn't distorted:
- **Not distorted**: range bars, 1m candles, `range_size` calibration
  (duplicates carry the same price, so OHLC and 1m-range are unchanged),
  and any run at `fill_probability=1.0`.
- **Distorted**: at `fill_probability < 1` the in-sample days get ~18%
  extra rolls on qualifying ticks, so the effective fill rate is
  systematically *higher* in-sample than out-of-sample. **This breaks
  condition 3 of `PASS_FAIL_CRITERION.md` (the p=0.7 stress test) — it is
  comparing two samples with different effective fill models.**
- **Distorted**: reversal exits (next-tick fill) can land on a duplicate
  print of the same price — marginally less adverse than reality.
- **Distorted**: `n_ticks` per bar, and therefore every flow metric in
  `diag_take_vs_rest.py` (ticks/bar, aggressor imbalance) on in-sample days.

Fix: one-time dedup pass over the v1 files, `.bak` alongside, then
**re-calibrate `range_size` and re-run every in-sample number**. Do not
compare a post-dedup number to a pre-dedup number.

## Strategy gaps (found by reading the code, not previously written down)

### S1. ~23% of sessions trade with no take-profit at all `OPEN`

`strategy.py:389-393` places the take order only on a **part1** fill. In a
part2-only session (666 of 2909 sessions on real BTC data) the position
runs to a reversal or the stop with no target. This is inherited behaviour,
not a bug, but it has never been named. Options: place the take on
whichever part fills first, or give part2 its own take.

### S2. `acceptance_bars` is only half-wired `OPEN`

It exists in `swings.check_break_and_acceptance` and in
`AcceptanceWindowStrategy`, but not in `WFStrategy.__init__` and not as a
`run_backtest.py` CLI flag. If the sweep shows it helps, this has to be
finished before the parameter can be used in a normal run.

### S3. Bias is an entry filter only, never an exit `OPEN`

Entry requires a bias; nothing exits on a bias flip — exits are
swing-break, take, or stop. Possibly correct (Beggs manages by structure),
but **`docs/BIAS_AUDIT_2026-07-07.md` Part B describes reversal exits as
closing positions "by a bias flip", which is factually wrong about our own
code** (they close on a swing break). Fix the doc before anyone reasons
from it.

### S4. The t-stat double-counts `OPEN`

part1 and part2 of the same session are two rows in `trades`, sharing one
exit event and one market regime. `metrics.py` already caveats that trades
aren't independent; the per-part split makes it worse. Options: report a
session-level aggregate (already named as the alternative in `metrics.py`'s
own docstring), or a block bootstrap over days.

### S5. `fill_probability` models a fair coin, not adverse selection `OPEN`

A resting limit fills *more* often precisely when price is running through
it against you. A uniform per-tick coin doesn't capture that skew, so the
stress test is optimistic in a way its own parameter can't express. Cheap
alternative to try: require price to trade through the level by ≥K ticks
before filling.

## Data / infrastructure gaps

### D1. One market, one week, one volatility regime `OPEN`

Every number in this project is BTC — the most bot-saturated market on the
venue. SOL / HYPE / XAU have been collecting live since 2026-07-03 (≈7
clean v2 days by now) and **`range_size` has never been calibrated for
them**. Ivan's liquidity hypothesis — that the edge, if any, lives on less
efficient markets — is exactly what `diag_take_vs_rest.py` was written to
test, and it has not been tested. Cheaper and more informative than a tenth
filter tuned on the same 8 BTC days.

### D2. Funding rates uncollected; holding time uninstrumented `OPEN`

Carried over from CONTEXT.md Next steps item 9, still true and still in the
right order: instrument mean holding time first, and only collect funding
history if that says swing-mode holds long enough to cross hourly
boundaries.

`market_ids: [0,1]` vs. the live `[0,1,2,24,92]`; BTC `range_size`
placeholder 5.00 vs. the calibrated 15.3. Known, cosmetic, listed here so
it stops being re-discovered.

## Suggested order of work

1. **A1 → A3** (hygiene). Nothing measured before this is trustworthy, and
   nothing measured after it is comparable to what came before.
2. **D1** (calibrate SOL/HYPE/XAU, run the same baseline). A horizontal
   check across markets beats another vertical filter on BTC. "No edge
   anywhere" and "no edge on BTC" are different conclusions.
3. **S-items / reversal-exit filters** (bias hold-time, `acceptance_bars`)
   — only once 1 and 2 are done, so a result means something.
4. **The honest fork.** If 1–3 come out negative: the mechanized WF subset,
   stripped of Beggs' discretionary components, has no edge. Then either
   add back some of what was removed (an order-flow filter — the aggressor
   imbalance in `diag_take_vs_rest.py` is a ready candidate), or record a
   negative research result and stop. For a learning project a documented
   negative result is a real outcome, and far better than advancing to
   paper trading on a number that was never clean.
